IoT Security Research Lab

Introduction

IoT or internet of things is the latest cutting edge technology and is expected to play a major role in the lives of human beings. It is expected that by 2020 there will be more than 50 billion devices connected to the internet.

This huge volume of devices will naturally lead to a major issue with security, and it becomes very essential to have a strong secure network built around these devices to prevent crackers from compromising systems.

Unlike comprising banks and credit cards, weak IoT systems can compromise entire cities and also defense, or infrastructure.

It is very essential that we need to develop a strong force who will safeguard our IoT related development from crackers.

Basic requirements

To get a good knowledge on IoT, it is essential for a student to have a good knowledge on the following,

  1. Sensors and their applications.
  2. Communication models like WiFi, blue tooth, Zigbee, Z-wave etc.
  3. Data collection and analysis.
  4. Security aspects of IoT networks.

Lab infrastructure

It is proposed to set up a comprehensive lab with facilities as mentioned in the excel sheet. Each lab can seat upto a maximum of 10 students per batch.

Proposed activities

  1. Research on developing “SMART” and secure IoT use cases for Government, Business and Consumer markets focusing on Sensors, microcontrollers, communication protocols and data bases with cloud integration.
  2. Provide a “hands on” lab environment for students and industry professionals to work on IoT and embedded projects, both existing and new in addition to conducting security exercises on the IoT networks.
  3. Providing advanced training- in house and onsite- on IoT and embedded systems using microcontrollers like,
    • Arduino
    • 8051 core
    • PIC
    • Raspberry PI
  4. Conducting periodic Arduino and Raspberry Pi Hackathon’s for educational and software development purposes.

Exercises

Students will learn to program using the various microcontrollers and communication modules. The exercises will start from simple LED’s to complex projects using Robotics and cloud integration

They will also learn to test the security aspects of each module they build, identify vulnerabilities and patch them accordingly.

Each exercise will be detailed in terms of schematic diagrams, BOM, code, and expected outputs.
